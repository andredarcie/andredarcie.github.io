How evolution works

Nobody programs these creatures. They start with random brains and get better on their own, through four simple rules repeated over and over:

  1. Variation — every creature is born with a different brain: a tiny neural network wired at random. No two think alike.
  2. Selection — each generation, the ones that get closest to the castle are kept; the rest are removed. That distance is their fitness.
  3. Heredity — the survivors become parents. Their brains are copied and combined, so good wiring is passed on to the next generation.
  4. Mutation — copies are never perfect: weights drift, and new neurons and links appear at random. Most changes hurt; the rare ones that help survive.
  5. Repeat — do this for many generations and behavior no one designed emerges: the swarm learns to dodge towers and home in on the sword.

You are the environment. The defenses you build are the selection pressure. Change them and evolution adapts to the new challenge — exactly like life adapts to its surroundings. There is no hidden training and no goal in any single brain: only survival of whoever happens to do a little better.
